Campus life

School church

was established in 1948

St. Francis Xavier School has a rich history that began with a decree in the mid 1950s by Cardinal Richard Cushing to build a larger church and a school on the current site on Pleasant Street in South Weymouth. The school began with just two grades, staffed by The Sisters of Divine Providence, and added a grade each year, until the first class of 8th Graders graduated in 1965. In the summer of 2020, our school began a partnership with Sacred Heart School in Weymouth. The Sacred Heart School campus is home to PreK through Grade 4. St. Francis Xavier School now includes students from grades 5–8. Students at St. Francis Xavier School proudly continue the school's traditions of family, parish and community.

School church

was established in 1980

Situated on the third floor of St. Xavier High School in the Jay Carroll Memorial Library, the Archives serve as the repository for the institutional records of St. Xavier High School. As such, the St. X Archives collects and preserves a myriad array of documents and memorabilia relating to the storied history of St. Xavier and the Long Blue Line. Doing so not only preserves the history of the institution for the current St. Xavier community but also ensures that future generations of Bombers will come to know and appreciate those who have come before them.
